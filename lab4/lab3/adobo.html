Adobo

  • Chicken or pork (or a combination of both)
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Bay leaves
  • Peppercorns

Cooking Procedure for Adobo...

Instructions: This is just one variation of adobo

  • In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces, soy sauce, minced garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  • In a large, deep skillet or pot, heat a small amount of cooking oil over medium heat.
  • Add the marinated chicken to the skillet and cook until the chicken turns brown on all sides.
  • Pour in the vinegar and allow it to simmer for a few minutes, allowing the strong vinegar smell to dissipate.
  • Add water to the skillet until it covers the chicken. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is tender. You can cover the skillet with a lid while simmering.
  • Taste the sauce and adjust the flavor by adding more soy sauce or vinegar according to your preference.
  • Continue to simmer until the sauce thickens and the chicken is fully cooked. You can also add a bit of sugar to balance the flavors, if desired.
  • Remove the bay leaves and peppercorns.
  • Serve the Chicken Adobo hot with steamed rice. You can garnish it with some chopped green onions for added flavor and presentation.
